Day 1

Arrival in Split

Sunday, May 18th, 2025

16:00: Depart London Gatwick (LGW) on easyJet EZY8599.

19:35: Arrive at Split Airport (SPU).

~20:00 - 21:00: Airport Transfer via Pleso Prijevoz Airport Shuttle bus to Split main bus station (Autobusni Kolodvor Split). Cost: ~€8 pp (cash often required). Duration: ~30-40 mins.

~21:00 - 21:30: Walk (10-15 mins) from the bus station to Art Heritage Hotel (Ulica Andrije Kačića Miošića 11A). Check-in closes at 23:00. Confirm luggage drop point beforehand.

Late Dinner (~22:00): Short walk from the hotel.

Primary Suggestion:

Villa Spiza (Kružićeva ul. 3, 4.7★) - Tiny, bustling spot known for its fresh, daily-changing menu of authentic Dalmatian dishes served straight from the kitchen. Sit-down, but very cosy/limited seating.

Often recommended: Whatever seafood is fresh, Pasticada (beef stew) if available, simple grilled fish.

Backup Suggestion:

Konoba Korta (Poljana Grgura Ninskog 3, 4.6★) - Charming courtyard setting slightly tucked away, offering traditional Dalmatian food with table service. A bit more relaxed than Villa Spiza.

Often recommended: Seafood risotto, grilled meats, tuna steak.

Day 2

Split Old Town & Sunset Views

Monday, May 19th, 2025

~10:30 onwards: Leisurely start. Grab coffee/pastries from a local 'pekara' (bakery).

Late Morning/Afternoon: Explore Diocletian's Palace complex (free to wander). See the Peristyle, Vestibule. Optional: Visit the Palace Substructures (~€6-€7 entry). Stroll along the Riva promenade.

Lunch (~14:00): Within the Palace walls.

Primary Suggestion:

Bokeria Kitchen & Wine (Domaldova ul. 8, 4.6★) - Stylish, modern restaurant with a vibrant atmosphere and well-presented Mediterranean dishes. Good for a slightly more upscale lunch.

Often recommended: Truffle pasta, tuna steak, burrata, charcuterie boards.

Backup Suggestion:

Uje Oil Bar (Dominisova ul. 3, 4.6★) - Focuses on high-quality local olive oils and ingredients. Offers Dalmatian tapas, platters, and mains in a rustic-chic setting.

Often recommended: Olive oil tasting platters, cheese boards, octopus salad, anchovies.

Late Afternoon/Sunset (~18:30 onwards): Walk up Marjan Hill. Aim for the Vidilica viewpoint cafe area just before sunset (around 20:00) for stunning golden hour views over the harbour and palace. (Note: Vidilica itself is a cafe, might be busy - the view is the main draw).

Optional Daytime Drink with View: Before heading up Marjan, consider Teraca Vidilica (the cafe at the viewpoint) or find a spot on the Riva like Olive Tree Vintage Caffe Bar (Trumbićeva obala 16, 4.5★) for sunshine and harbour views (check recent reviews for vibe).

Dinner (~20:30/21:00): Back down near the Palace/Old Town.

Primary Suggestion:

ARTiČOK (Ul. bana Josipa Jelačića 3, 4.7★) - Modern European cuisine with creative flair in a pleasant setting. Good service, often requires booking.

Often recommended: Beef cheeks, tuna tartare, seasonal specials.

Backup Suggestion:

Ma:Toni (Prilaz braće Kaliterna 6, 4.7★) - Slightly further walk (5-10 mins from main palace area), but highly regarded for its refined Mediterranean food and intimate atmosphere.

Often recommended: Black risotto, lamb shank, fish dishes.

Night (~22:30 onwards): Cocktails nearby.

Primary Suggestion (Craft/Vibe):

The Daltonist Craft Bar (Hrvojeva 10, 4.7★) - Known for creative, well-executed cocktails, knowledgeable staff, and a cool, welcoming vibe. Often praised for its atmosphere.

Backup Suggestion (Speakeasy Style):

Noor Fine Spirits & Cocktails (Bajamontijeva ul. 2, 4.5★) - Intimate, darker ambiance, focusing on high-quality spirits and classic/craft cocktails. Good for a quieter, more focused drink experience.

Day 3

Ferry to Hvar

Tuesday, May 20th, 2025

Morning: Relaxed morning, check out from Art Heritage Hotel. Perhaps grab a coffee on the Riva.

~10:45/11:00: Walk to Split Ferry Port (Gat Svetog Duje).

Ferry to Hvar (~11:30 - 12:30): Aim for an earlier fast catamaran (foot passengers only) to Hvar Town harbour to maximise your time there. Operators: Jadrolinija, Krilo (Kapetan Luka), TP Line. Book online IN ADVANCE! Cost: ~€6-€26 pp. Duration: ~1 hour.

~12:30 - 13:30: Arrive Hvar Town harbour. Walk uphill (~10-15 mins) to Apartments Teo 1 (Šime Buzolića Tome 1 - check location). Check in (from 14:00 - confirm luggage drop if arriving earlier).

Afternoon: Explore Hvar Town: St. Stephen's Square (Pjaca), Cathedral, Arsenal building, wander the charming narrow streets.

Optional Daytime Drink with View: Adriana Roof Top Bar (Fabrika 28, 4.6★) at the Adriana Hotel offers great harbour views and sunshine (can be pricey/busy). Alternatively, Teraca Bar (Trg Sv. Stjepana, 4.8★) overlooks the main square, excellent for people-watching with a glass of local white wine (Pošip, Grk).

Dinner (~20:30): In Hvar Town.

Primary Suggestion:

Dalmatino (Ul. Sveti Marak 1, 4.8★) - Very popular, known for excellent service, lively atmosphere, and well-regarded Dalmatian dishes with a modern twist. Booking essential.

Often recommended: Gnocchi (especially steak or truffle), fresh fish platters, Gregada (fish stew).

Backup Suggestion:

Black Pepper Restaurant (Skaline od Gojave 11, 4.6★) - Romantic setting on stepped alleyways, offering creative Mediterranean cuisine with a focus on presentation and flavour combinations.

Often recommended: Tuna steak, lamb shank, creative starters, tasting menus.

Night (~22:30 onwards): Cocktails nearby.

Primary Suggestion (Craft/Atmosphere):

Ka'lavanda Music Bar (Dr. Mate Miličića 7, 4.5★ - Note: Rating can fluctuate, check recent reviews) - Chic alleyway bar known for good cocktails, music (can vary, sometimes DJs), and a lively but sophisticated vibe. Fits the stylish criteria.

Backup Suggestion (View/Hotel Bar):

Adriana Roof Top Bar (Fabrika 28, 4.6★) - Revisited for evening cocktails. Offers stunning harbour views, usually a relaxed but trendy atmosphere. Cocktails are generally good quality hotel standard.

Day 4

Hvar Views, Beach or Blue Cave

Wednesday, May 21st, 2025

Option 1: Fortress & Beach Day

~10:30 onwards: Leisurely start.

Late Morning/Lunch: Head to a beach. Option A: Walk east (~20-25 mins) to Pokonji Dol Beach (pebble beach). Option B: Take a Water Taxi from Hvar harbour to the Pakleni Islands (e.g., Palmižana, Jerolim) for stunning bays. Cost: ~€8-€11 return pp. Have a casual lunch at a beach bar (e.g., Laganini Lounge Bar & Fish House on Palmižana - pricey, check vibe/menu) or back in town.

Late Afternoon/Sunset (~18:30 onwards): Hike up to the Spanish Fortress (Tvrdava Fortica) (~20-30 mins walk) for spectacular panoramic views during golden hour (sunset ~20:00). Essential photo spot. Entry: ~€10 pp.

Option 2: Blue Cave & Island Hopping Tour

Alternatively, dedicate today to a full-day speedboat tour. Most tours visit the Blue Cave (Biševo island), Green Cave, Stiniva Cove (Vis island), and often include swimming stops in the Pakleni Islands. This is a fantastic photography opportunity but takes the whole day (approx. 8-10 hours). Book in advance online or via kiosks in Hvar harbour the day before. Cost: Varies, ~€80-€120+ pp.

Dinner (~20:30/21:00): Back in Hvar Town.

Primary Suggestion:

Fig Hvar (Stjepana Papafave 35, 4.7★) - Casual, popular spot known for its fresh, flavourful dishes with global influences (often Mediterranean/Californian fusion). Good for a relaxed but tasty meal.

Often recommended: Pulled pork sandwich, fig & ricotta flatbread, breakfast/brunch items (if open).

Backup Suggestion:

Konoba Menego (Kroz Grodu 26, 4.6★) - Traditional family-run 'konoba' in a charming stone building, offering classic Dalmatian dishes in an authentic setting.

Often recommended: Peka (needs pre-order usually), grilled fish/meat platters, homemade pasta.

Night: Enjoy more cocktails! Perhaps revisit a favourite or try the other suggestion from Day 3.

Day 5

Hvar to Trogir

Thursday, May 22nd, 2025

Morning: Check out from Apartments Teo 1. Take a pre-booked catamaran back to Split Ferry Port (~1 hour). Aim for a morning departure.

Direct Ferry Check: Monitor the Bura Line website. If their seasonal Hvar-Milna-Trogir route is running, booking this would be more direct and avoid changing in Split.

Transport Split -> Trogir: Walk from Split Ferry Port to the adjacent Split Main Bus Station. Take Bus Line 37 to Trogir Bus Station (departs from Sukoišan station nearby, or catch it along its route - check Promet Split schedule). Cost: ~€2-€3 cash. Duration: ~50 mins. (Alternatively, intercity buses like FlixBus/Arriva are quicker but might be less frequent/convenient from the port).

Arrival & Check-in: Arrive Trogir Bus Station, walk to Studio Makala (Šubićeva 5) and check in (from 14:00). Re-confirm your very early check-out plan for tomorrow! Ask about luggage drop point.

Afternoon/Evening: Explore UNESCO Trogir Old Town. Visit the Cathedral of St. Lawrence. Consider climbing the tower (~€5 entry) around 16:30-17:00 for great late-afternoon light on Kamerlengo Fortress. See Kamerlengo Fortress (Entry ~€3.5-€5). Stroll the Riva (promenade).

Optional Daytime Drink with View: Find a cafe along the Trogir Riva (promenade) facing the water for sunshine and boat views. Many options here, check ratings locally (e.g., Corte Bar - check recent reviews).

Dinner in Trogir (~20:30): Within the Old Town.

Primary Suggestion:

Konoba Trs (Ul. Matije Gupca 14, 4.7★) - Highly-rated, intimate restaurant known for its elevated take on traditional Dalmatian cuisine, good service, and pleasant atmosphere. Often requires booking.

Often recommended: Tuna steak, seafood pasta/risotto, lamb chops, steak dishes.

Backup Suggestion:

Restaurant Don Dino (Ul. Blaža Jurjeva Trogiranina 4, 4.7★) - Another popular choice offering a mix of seafood and meat dishes with modern presentation in a nice setting within the old town walls.

Often recommended: Scallops, sea bass, steak, pasta dishes.

Night (~22:30 onwards): Cocktails nearby.

Primary Suggestion (Views/Atmosphere):

Bubalus Burger Bar (Obala bana Berislavića 15, 4.7★) - While primarily a burger place, it's often praised for its good cocktails, location on the Riva, and lively but relaxed evening atmosphere. Worth checking for drinks.

Backup Suggestion (Simple/Riva):

Kamerlengo Bar (Near the fortress entrance) - More of a standard cafe/bar but enjoys a prime location near the fortress and Riva, good for a simpler drink while soaking in the atmosphere.

Preparation & Key Tips

Booking & Admin

  • Flights: Pre-book extra cabin baggage & seat selection with easyJet now for camera gear.
  • Ferries: Book Split-Hvar & Hvar-Split catamarans (Jadrolinija, Krilo, TP Line) online ASAP when schedules release (Jan/Feb).
  • Accommodation: Contact hosts now re: check-in/out, luggage drop points, contact details. Confirm Trogir early check-out.
  • Bura Line Watch: Check Bura Line from Feb/Mar for potential direct Hvar-Trogir route.
  • Restaurant Bookings: Consider booking dinner spots like Dalmatino, Trs, Don Dino, ARTiČOK, Ma:Toni in advance, especially for specific times.

Comfort & Logistics

  • Footwear: Pack comfy trail trainers for grip on limestone.
  • Hydration & Gout: Stay hydrated; consider electrolyte tablets.
  • Cash: Carry ~€150 cash (Euros) in small notes for buses, small purchases, tips. Most restaurants/bars take cards.
  • Luggage: Pack light; use Split luggage storage if needed between hotel and ferry.

Photography

  • Golden Hour: Plan for sunset shots (Marjan Hill, Hvar Fortress, Trogir Tower). Sunset ~20:00.
  • Gear: Remember chargers, batteries, cards!
